The Goal

Claim as many squares as you can.

  Rules

About The Board

  • The game is played on a 7 by 7 board.
  • There are 49 squares in total.
  • There are 4 tiles that are inaccessible. Those are (2, 2), (2, 4), (4, 2) and (4, 4).
  • Each player has two pieces in the board.
  • Green player's pieces are at (1, 1) and (5, 5).
  • Orange player's pieces are at (1,5) and (5, 1).

How to Claim The Square

In each turn a player can make the following moves to one of his pieces:

  • Move

    • A piece can be moved to one of the four adjacent empty squares(Up, Down, Right, Left)
    • When moving, the player loses the claim of the initial square.

  • Replicate

    • A piece can be replicated to one of the four diagonal squares(Top-right, Top-left, Bottom-right, Bottom-left).
    • When replicating, the player will have both the initial square and the destination square.

  • Convert

    Moving and Replicating are the simplest way to claim a square. There's also another way to convert opponent's squares.
    • When moving or replicating a piece, if the position that the piece is moving/replicating to has any of its 4 neighbors (Right, Left, Up, Down) claimed by the opponent, those squares are converted.

Score

Player's score is calculated based on the number of pieces the player has on the board.

End Condition

  • All of the cells of the 7 by 7 board is filled.
  • One of the player runs out of moves. The empty squares will be filled with pieces of the other player.
  • After 200 moves the game will be forcefully ended. Player with the most pieces will be declared as winner.

  Game Input

Initilization Input

Line 1: one integer playerIndex - PlayerIndex 1 means opponent's index will 2 and vice versa.

For Each Game Loop

Line 1: one integer cellCount - Number of cells in the board.

Next cellCount lines: three integers row col state - Position and state of a cell in the board.
  • State -1: Cell is blocked.
  • State 0: Cell is empty.
  • State 1: Cell has a piece of player with index 1.
  • State 2: Cell has a piece of player with index 2.
Output
A single line containing player's move. Example: 5 1 REPL 6 2.
  • r1 c1 MOVE r2 c2: player's piece at row r1 and column c1 moves to row r2 and column c2. Cell (r1, c1) becomes empty.
  • r1 c1 REPL r2 c2: player's piece at row r1 and column c1 replicates to row r2 and column c2.
You must provide a valid command for exactly one piece each turn.
Constraints
playerIndex is either 1 or 2.
cellCount is 49.
0≤row≤6
0≤col≤6
-1≤state≤2
Response time per turn ≤ 100ms
